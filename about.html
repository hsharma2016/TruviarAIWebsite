The Story

Why I Started Truviar

What I Kept Hearing

The pattern was always the same. Firm wins a big engagement, team goes into delivery, pipeline activity stops completely. Engagement wraps up, pipeline is empty again. So they hustle for a few weeks — some LinkedIn posting, a few cold emails, working the network. Maybe a referral comes through. Cycle resets. Repeat indefinitely.

The frustrating part wasn't that this was hard to fix. It was that these founders already knew what was wrong. They needed a system for new business development — one that ran whether they were busy or not. The knowledge was there. The time and infrastructure weren't. And building it themselves meant pulling focus from the actual work that paid the bills.

So I Built the Solution

Truviar is the answer to that gap. A growth system designed around precisely targeting the right decision-makers, engaging them through AI-assisted outreach, qualifying conversations before they reach you, and reporting honestly on everything. Not a campaign you run for three months and replace. Infrastructure you build, improve, and run on.

The outcome is straightforward: your calendar fills with qualified conversations — consistently — and you stop spending mental energy on where the next client is coming from. You focus on the work. We handle the pipeline.

How We Think

What We Actually Believe

Systems Over Guesswork

If your growth depends on you remembering to do outreach when things get quiet, that's not a system. We build something that runs whether you're busy or not.

Measure Everything

We track what's working and what isn't. You get honest numbers, not activity reports dressed up to look impressive.

Your Time Is the Constraint

You're the most valuable person in your firm. The system should protect your time, not consume it. If it requires more than a monthly review and showing up to conversations, something's wrong.

Precision Over Volume

Reaching the right 300 people with a message that resonates will always beat blasting 3,000 strangers with something generic.
